Welcome to the Department of Computer Science. The department offers BS, MS, and PhD programs in Computer Science; MS programs in Software Engineering, Information Systems, and Information Security and Assurance; and a BS program in Applied Computer Science with various concentrations. The department also offers concentrations in the inter-disciplinary MS in Data Analytics Engineering and the PhD in Information Technology offered by the Volgenau School of Engineering.

The Department is part of the Volgenau School of Engineering at George Mason University.

Computer Science, MS

The graduate program leading to an MS in Computer Science includes both fundamentals and advanced work in the areas of artificial intelligence and databases, programming languages and software engineering, systems and networks, theoretical computer science, and visual computing. Graduate classes are divided into basic classes with no prerequisite and advanced classes, which have prerequisites.

Admission Requirements

In addition to fulfilling Mason's admission requirements for graduate study, applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Hold a baccalaureate degree.
  • Earned a cumulative GPA of 3.00.
  • Submit transcripts of all post secondary education.
  • A self assessment form.
  • Three letters of recommendation.
  • An official GRE score.

Degree Requirements (30 credits)

Completion of the MS in CS requires 30 credits of graduate courses. Courses are grouped in the following five broad areas: Artificial Intelligence and Databases, Programming Languages and Software Engineering, Systems and Networks, Theoretical Computer Science, and Visual Computing. The list of preapproved courses with their areas is provided below.
